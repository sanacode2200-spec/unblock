iPhone videos can be perfectly fine and still fail on a PC. The problem is usually the format Windows apps expect, not the video itself.

MOV files are common on iPhone, but many Windows workflows expect MP4.
HEVC / H.265 can trigger missing codec errors on Windows.
HDR video may look washed out or too bright on normal displays.
Large 4K files can be slow to send, upload, or import into editors.

FAQ

iPhone video compatibility questions

Why won't my iPhone video play on Windows?

It may use MOV, HEVC, HDR, or another format that the Windows app does not support by default. Unblock converts it to a Windows-friendly MP4.

How do I convert iPhone MOV to MP4 on Windows?

Drop the MOV file into Unblock. It detects the video and creates an MP4 output automatically.

What is HEVC and why does Windows complain about it?

HEVC is an efficient video codec used by newer iPhones. Some Windows installs need extra codec support to play it, so Unblock changes it to H.264.

Can Unblock convert HEVC to H.264?

Yes. The default Windows MP4 output uses H.264 video and AAC audio for broad compatibility.

Why does my iPhone HDR video look washed out on Windows?

HDR video can display incorrectly on SDR screens or apps. Unblock can make an SDR MP4 so the result looks normal.

Does Unblock keep my original video?

Yes. The original file is left untouched and the Windows-friendly MP4 is written as a new file.

Does Unblock upload my video to the cloud?

No. The product direction is local processing on your computer, which is better for private videos.

Can I batch convert many iPhone videos?

Yes. The converter screen is designed for multiple files with a compact queue and one-click conversion.

Does Unblock work with 4K videos?

Yes. 4K is supported, with an optional smaller-file preset for sharing.

Is this better than installing a codec?

Installing a codec may only fix playback on one PC. Converting to MP4 makes the file easier to share, import, and use elsewhere.
